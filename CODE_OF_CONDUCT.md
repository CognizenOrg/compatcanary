# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in CompatCanary welcoming, safe, and constructive for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience, or ability.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific.
- Critique behavior, evidence, and code rather than people.
- Assume good intent while remaining open to correction.
- Protect credentials, private endpoints, prompts, and confidential response data.
- Disclose relevant affiliations when discussing providers, runtimes, or paid services.
- Accept responsibility for mistakes and help repair their impact.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Deliberately misleading compatibility claims, fabricated evidence, or undisclosed promotion.
- Sustained disruption of project spaces or maintainer decisions.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, Discussions, reviews, and other spaces where someone represents the CompatCanary project.

## Reporting and enforcement

For ordinary disagreements, pause and return to reproducible technical evidence. For a conduct concern that can be discussed publicly, open an issue and ask for maintainer review. For threats, private-information exposure, or other sensitive abuse, use GitHub's private reporting and abuse-reporting tools rather than posting details publicly.

Maintainers may edit or remove content and may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban participants whose behavior is unsafe or persistently disruptive. Enforcement decisions should consider context, impact, repair, and the safety of affected people.
